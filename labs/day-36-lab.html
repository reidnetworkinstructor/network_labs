Wi-Fi Sleuth: Wireless Recon and Rogue Device Scanning

Objective: Use free tools to scan Wi-Fi environments, identify encryption methods, detect weak networks, and simulate rogue AP awareness.

ðŸ›  Tools Needed:

  • Wigle.net account (free)
  • Wigle WiFi App (Android only) OR laptop with built-in Wi-Fi scanner
  • Optional (Mac/Linux): iwlist, airport, or nmcli

Step 1: Local Wi-Fi Scan

  • If you're on Windows: download WiFiInfoView
  • Scan all available SSIDs in your area
  • Document at least 5 networks: SSID, signal strength, channel, encryption type
  • Bonus: Can you spot any open or WEP networks?

Step 2: Mobile Recon with Wigle

  • Install the Wigle WiFi app (Android) and walk around your block
  • Record SSIDs with:
    • Weak encryption (e.g., WEP, None)
    • High signal strength (possible rogue APs nearby)
    • Strange or suspicious names (e.g., "Free_Public_WiFi", "xfinity_wifi")
  • Log your data in a small table

Step 3: Spot the Rogue

  • Review all SSIDs. Look for:
    • Identical SSID to your home/work network but on a different channel
    • Open networks mimicking legit names
    • Duplicate MAC addresses
  • Reflection: Would you trust any of these? How would an attacker trick you?

Step 4: Research AP Hardening

  • Look up best practices for securing home Wi-Fi networks:
  • Examples:
    • Disable WPS
    • Use WPA3 (or at least WPA2-AES)
    • Hide SSID broadcast (controversial)
    • Limit DHCP pool / use MAC filtering
  • Write a brief list: 3 things you could change today to harden your own Wi-Fi
