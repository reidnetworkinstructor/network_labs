Day 1 Lab

Map a Packet’s Journey Through the OSI Model

Objective: Trace the path of a packet as it moves through the 7 layers of the OSI model when accessing a website like google.com.

Tools Needed: Pen and paper or a free diagramming tool (e.g., draw.io).

Step 1: Scenario Setup

Imagine this situation: You open your browser and type in google.com. What happens behind the scenes as that request moves through the network?

Step 2: Trace the Packet Through the OSI Layers

  • Layer 7 (Application): Your browser (e.g., Chrome) creates an HTTP(S) request to access google.com.
  • Layer 6 (Presentation): The request is formatted and encrypted using TLS for HTTPS so it can be transmitted securely.
  • Layer 5 (Session): A session is established between your browser and Google’s server to manage and maintain the conversation.
  • Layer 4 (Transport): TCP segments the data, assigns ports (e.g., 443 for HTTPS), and ensures reliable delivery with acknowledgments.
  • Layer 3 (Network): IP addresses are used for routing. Your gateway/router forwards packets toward the destination server’s IP.
  • Layer 2 (Data Link): Frames are built with MAC addresses and sent over your local network (Wi‑Fi or Ethernet).
  • Layer 1 (Physical): Bits travel as electrical signals, light, or radio waves across the physical medium.

Step 3: Visualize the Journey

Create a simple diagram of the path:

  • Path: Your Computer → Router/Gateway → Internet → Google’s Server
  • Label what each OSI layer is doing at each hop.
  • Use arrows/boxes to show flow from request to response.

Portfolio Tip: Export your diagram as an image or PDF and include it in an ePortfolio entry explaining each OSI layer’s role.
