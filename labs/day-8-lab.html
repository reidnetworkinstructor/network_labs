Analyze Network Traffic with Wireshark

Capture HTTP, HTTPS, and DNS traffic to see ports and protocols in action.

Objective

Use Wireshark to capture and analyze network traffic, observing how application-layer protocols like HTTP, HTTPS, and DNS use specific ports, and identify the role of ports in communication.

Tool Needed

Wireshark (free, download from wireshark.org) — a network protocol analyzer for capturing and analyzing traffic.

Step 1: Install and Set Up Wireshark

  1. Download Wireshark and install it on your computer (you may also be prompted to install Npcap).
  2. Open Wireshark and select your active network interface (e.g., Wi-Fi or Ethernet).
  3. Click the green shark fin icon to begin capturing packets.

Step 2: Generate and Capture HTTP Traffic

  1. In your browser, visit a non-secure site using http:// (e.g., http://example.com).
  2. Stop the capture in Wireshark after the page loads (red square icon).
  3. In the filter bar, type tcp.port == 80 and press Enter.
  4. Look for packets with “HTTP” in the Protocol column and inspect their source/destination ports.
  5. Note: “HTTP traffic on port 80, source port [random], destination port 80.”

Step 3: Generate and Capture HTTPS and DNS Traffic

  1. Start a new capture.
  2. Visit https://google.com in your browser.
  3. Stop the capture.
  4. Filter for HTTPS: tcp.port == 443. Look for “TLS” packets.
  5. Filter for DNS: udp.port == 53. Look for “DNS” packets with queries and responses.
  6. Note: “HTTPS traffic on port 443, DNS query for google.com on port 53.”

Step 4: Reflect

Write a paragraph answering: How did Wireshark help you understand the role of ports in network communication? What did you learn about protocol-port relationships?

Hint: HTTP on port 80, HTTPS on 443, DNS on 53 — ports route the traffic
