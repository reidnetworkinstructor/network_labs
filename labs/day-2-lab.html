Day 2 Lab

Design Star and Mesh Topologies

Objective: Use draw.io to design two network topologies—star and mesh—for a small business, and compare their suitability for LAN vs WAN scenarios.

Tools Needed: draw.io (diagrams.net) — free, browser-based diagramming tool.

Step 1: Set Up draw.io

  • Open draw.io and create a new blank diagram.
  • Either split one canvas vertically or create two separate diagrams: one for the star topology, one for the mesh topology.

Step 2: Star Topology for a Small Business LAN

  • Scenario: 1 router, 1 server, and 3 workstations.
  • Place a central box labeled Router.
  • Add icons/boxes for Server and three Workstations around it.
  • Connect each device to the router with straight lines (Ethernet links).
  • Label the diagram: Small Business LAN — Star Topology.

Step 3: Mesh Topology for a Small Business WAN

  • Scenario: 4 branch office routers (Branch 1–4).
  • Arrange four router boxes in a square/circle layout.
  • Connect every router to every other router (6 total links).
  • Label the diagram: Small Business WAN — Mesh Topology.

Step 4: Compare and Reflect

Write a short paragraph comparing the two designs:

  • Why is a star topology typically better for LANs?
  • Why is a mesh topology more appropriate for WANs?
  • Hint: Star is simple, low-cost, and easy to manage; mesh offers redundancy and high availability for critical inter-site links.

Export both diagrams as PNG or PDF (File → Export) to include in your portfolio.
