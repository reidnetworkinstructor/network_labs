Design Star and Mesh Topologies

Use Draw.io to visualize small business LAN and WAN architectures

Objective

Use Draw.io to design two network topologies—star and mesh—for a small business, and compare their suitability for a LAN and WAN scenario.

Tool Needed

Draw.io (diagrams.net) — free, browser-based diagramming tool.

Step 1: Set Up Draw.io

  • Open draw.io and create a new blank diagram.
  • Split the canvas vertically or create two separate diagrams: one for the star topology, one for the mesh topology.

Step 2: Star Topology for a Small Business LAN

  • Scenario: 1 router, 1 server, and 3 workstations.
  • Drag a rectangle labeled “Router” to the center.
  • Surround it with 4 devices (use icons: server and computers).
  • Connect each device to the router using straight lines (representing Ethernet).
  • Label this diagram: “Small Business LAN - Star Topology”.

Step 3: Mesh Topology for a Small Business WAN

  • Scenario: 4 branch office routers.
  • Place 4 labeled boxes (Branch 1 Router, Branch 2 Router, etc.) in a square or circle layout.
  • Connect every router to every other router (6 total connections).
  • Label the diagram: “Small Business WAN - Mesh Topology”.

Step 4: Compare and Reflect

Write a paragraph comparing the two designs:

  • Why is star topology better for a LAN?
  • Why is mesh more appropriate for WAN?
  • Hint: Star is simple, low-cost, and easy to manage; mesh provides full redundancy and high availability, ideal for critical WAN links.

Export both diagrams as PNG or PDF (File > Export) to include in your portfolio.
