Day 11 Lab: Crimp and Test an Ethernet Cable

Objective

Build a straight-through Ethernet cable and test it in both the real world and simulation, so you understand how physical Layer 1 connections come together.

Tools Needed

  • UTP cable (Cat 5e or Cat 6)
  • 2x RJ45 connectors
  • Crimping tool
  • Wire stripper
  • Optional: Network tester
  • Optional: Cisco Packet Tracer

Step 1: Prep the Cable

Cut 1–2 meters of UTP cable. Use your wire stripper to remove about 2 inches of the outer jacket. You should see four twisted color pairs inside.

Untwist and arrange the wires into the T568B standard:

Orange-White, Orange, Green-White, Blue, Blue-White, Green, Brown-White, Brown

Line the wires up flat and trim them evenly to about half an inch long. Slide them into an RJ45 connector with the clip side down. Orange-white should be on the left (Pin 1).

Step 2: Crimp It

Push the cable fully into the RJ45 connector until each wire touches the end. Double-check that the wires stayed in order.

Place the connector into the crimping tool and give it a firm squeeze.

Repeat this process for the other end of the cable—use the same T568B pinout to make a straight-through cable.

Check that the jacket is secured under the locking tab, and all the copper pins are flush and visible through the connector.

Step 3: Physical Test

Plug one end of the cable into a computer and the other into a switch or router. Do you see link lights blinking? Try accessing the internet or pinging your router.

If you have a cable tester, plug both ends into the tester. You should see all lights activate in sequence if it's wired correctly.

Example result: “Crimped Cat 6 cable — link established with router successfully.”

Step 4: Simulate It (Optional)

In Cisco Packet Tracer:

  • Add two PCs and a switch.
  • Use the “copper straight-through” cable tool to connect both PCs to the switch.
  • Assign IPs: PC0 = 192.168.1.10, PC1 = 192.168.1.11
  • Ping from PC0 to PC1.
Result: “Simulated RJ45 cable working — ping reply received.”

Step 5: Reflect

Write a short reflection on what you just did. Answer these questions:

  • How does a properly crimped cable support Layer 1 communication?
  • Why is it important to use the T568B standard on both ends?

Hint: Standardized wiring ensures that signals flow correctly between devices. Without it, the physical layer connection won’t work at all.
