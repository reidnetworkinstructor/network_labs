Day 88 Toolkit

Interview Mastery Resources

Purpose: Ready-to-use templates, prompts, and scripts to apply the 3 C’s — Clear, Concise, Confident — in every interview.

1. STAR Story Templates

Use these outlines to prep for behavioral questions. Keep each section 1–2 sentences.

Prompt Example Template
A time you solved a problem quickly S: Describe the problem and who was affected.
T: Define your role/responsibility.
A: Steps you took, why you chose them.
R: Measurable outcome and takeaway.
A time you learned a tool fast S: Urgency for learning the tool.
T: Your deliverable or deadline.
A: How you studied/practiced.
R: How it impacted the project or team.

2. Common Technical Walkthrough Skeletons

Outline both high-level and detailed answers for each scenario.

  • Network Triage:
    • Confirm scope (one user or many)
    • Check basic connectivity (ping, tracert)
    • Verify IP/DNS settings
    • Escalate or document resolution
  • Log Analysis:
    • Identify log source and timeframe
    • Search/filter for relevant entries
    • Look for anomalies or error patterns
    • Summarize findings clearly
  • Account Lockout:
    • Verify user identity
    • Check logs for failed login patterns
    • Identify source (brute force, user error, config issue)
    • Reset or unlock with security measures

3. Bridging Requirement Gaps — Script Examples

Use the Acknowledge–Relate–Plan formula.

Gap Bridge Example
No production experience with Jamf Acknowledge: "I haven’t used Jamf in production yet."
Relate: "I’ve managed device policies in Intune and M365."
Plan: "I’d follow vendor docs, spin up a lab, and shadow an experienced teammate to be effective within two weeks."
No direct firewall management Acknowledge: "I haven’t configured firewalls end-to-end."
Relate: "I’ve troubleshot blocked ports and created rules under supervision."
Plan: "I’d review configuration guides and practice in a lab before touching production."

4. Practice Prompts

  • Explain your favorite Hoodie Challenge lab in 60 seconds.
  • Describe a time you solved a network issue with minimal tools.
  • Walk through how you’d handle a mass account lockout.
  • Share a time you had to quickly learn a new platform.
  • Give an example of improving documentation or a process.

5. Pre-Interview Warm-Up Plan

  1. Review your top 3 STAR stories.
  2. Rehearse one technical walkthrough.
  3. Read a bridging script out loud to normalize the language.
  4. Check your notes on the company and role.
  5. Do a 2-minute voice warm-up to reduce tension.
