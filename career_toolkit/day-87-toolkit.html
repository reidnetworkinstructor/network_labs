Day 87 Toolkit

Job Hunt Strategy

Skill‑first search strings, outreach scripts, and a lightweight tracker template. Apply even if you don’t meet 100% of “requirements.”

Pair with: Day 87 Checklist

Use the Copy buttons to paste strings into job boards, notes, or your tracker.

1) Skill‑Based Search Strings (LinkedIn / Indeed / Google)

("CompTIA Network+" OR Network+) AND ("Active Directory" OR "ticketing system" OR troubleshooting)
("Security+" OR "CompTIA Security+") AND (SIEM OR Splunk OR "log analysis" OR "incident response")
("AZ-900" OR "Azure Fundamentals") AND ("Microsoft 365" OR "Azure AD" OR identity OR helpdesk)
("TCP/IP" OR DNS OR DHCP) AND ("help desk" OR "it support" OR "service desk")

2) Filters to Toggle (per board)

Date posted: Past 24 hours / Past week
Experience: Entry level / Associate
Job type: Full-time, Contract-to-hire
Work setting: On-site, Hybrid, Remote (check all that apply)
Company: Include smaller orgs, MSPs, universities, hospitals
Exclude terms: "Senior", "Lead", "Architect" (use job board exclude filters)

3) “Apply Anyway” Mindset Prompts

If I match ~70% of bullets (especially the first few), I apply.
If a “requirement” is learnable in weeks, I apply.
If I have proof-of-work (labs/projects) that map to the role, I apply.
Red flags worth skipping: clearly senior title, heavy vendor years-of-experience requirements you cannot bridge, or niche stacks unrelated to your target path.

4) Tailoring Quick‑Swap Blocks (Paste into ATS version)

SUMMARY: Entry-level IT professional (Network+, AZ-900) with hands-on labs in [SKILL]. Targeting roles that involve [KEY RESPONSIBILITY] and [PLATFORM/TOOL].
SKILLS (top line): TCP/IP, DNS, Windows Admin, Microsoft 365, Azure (AZ‑900), ServiceNow, Splunk (log analysis)
BULLET SWAP: Resolved 30+ tickets/week across Windows/M365; cut resolution time by 25% via triage & documentation aligned to [TOOL/PROCESS IN JD].
PROJECT SWAP: Tiny Log Analyzer — speeds failed‑login triage (<2m). Relevance: SIEM/log analysis focus in this role.

5) Recruiter Outreach & Follow‑Up

Subject: Quick intro — Entry-level [Role]
Hi [Name], I applied to [Job Title, Job ID] at [Company]. I’m Network+/AZ-900 with labs in [skill]. Portfolio: [link]. Happy to share more context if helpful—thanks for your time.
Follow-up (7–10 days):
Hi [Name] — checking in on my application for [Job Title]. I can speak to [requirement from JD] and have relevant projects here: [link]. Appreciate any next steps you’d recommend.

6) Daily & Weekly Rhythm

Daily (30–45m): Run 3 skill searches → save best roles → tailor & apply to 2–3 → log in tracker → send 1–2 outreach notes.
Weekly: Refresh resume keywords, add any new project links, rotate search strings, follow up on pending apps.

8) Company Watchlist (Prospecting)

Target companies: local MSPs, hospitals, universities, SaaS startups, city/county roles, K‑12 districts, retail HQs.
Action: Follow each on LinkedIn, set job alerts for “IT Support”, “Security Analyst”, “Technology Specialist”.

9) Interview Prep Trigger

When an interview is scheduled:
• Re-read the JD; highlight top 5 responsibilities
• Map 1 project or bullet to each responsibility
• Prepare a 30–45s story (STAR) for 2 successes + 1 challenge
• Print your one‑page resume and bring it

10) Links You’ll Reuse Often

  • Profile LinkedIn / GitHub / Portfolio — ensure all links are current and consistent
  • Resume One‑Page PDF (for recruiter email) + ATS DOCX (for portals)
  • Projects 2–3 best GitHub repos or case‑study pages
