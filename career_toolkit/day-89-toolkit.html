Day 89 Toolkit

Mock Interview Day

Objective: Equip yourself with the scripts, prompts, and scoring tools needed to run a realistic and effective mock interview.

1. Mock Interview Scoring Rubric

Category Needs Work (1–2) Adequate (3) Strong (4–5)
Clarity Jargon, meandering Understandable Direct, plain language
Concision Rambling, repeats On time Tight, headline first
Confidence Apologies, hedging Neutral Calm, assured tone
STAR Use Missing Result Full but long Crisp, strong Result
Technical Depth Only high-level Some detail Proper depth shifts
Relevance Off-role stories Mostly aligned Laser-aligned examples

2. Recruiter Screen Script

  1. Tell me about yourself — 60 seconds, focus on current skills and role alignment.
  2. Why this company and role? — 45–60 seconds, connect to mission and recent projects.
  3. Project highlight — 60–90 seconds, outcome-based with a measurable result.
  4. General capability question — choose from multitasking, communication, or quick learning scenarios.

3. Technical Scenario Prompts

  • Help Desk / IT Support: User cannot access a shared drive after a password reset.
  • SOC / Security: Overnight spike in failed logins from unknown IP ranges.
  • Cloud Support (Azure): User blocked by new Conditional Access policy.

4. Behavioral Question Bank (STAR)

  • Describe a time you solved a tough problem under pressure.
  • Describe a time you made a mistake and how you handled it.
  • Give an example of working with a difficult teammate or client.

5. Fix-it Loop Templates

Use these drills to quickly improve weak areas identified in your rubric:

  • Clarity: Rewrite your first sentence as a headline; record until it’s sharp.
  • Concision: Deliver each answer within a timer window (55–65 seconds).
  • Confidence: Replace hedging language (“maybe,” “probably”) with assertive statements.
  • STAR Brevity: Limit S/T to 2 sentences, Action to 3, Result to 1.
  • Technical Depth: Prepare 3 low-level details for each high-level summary.
